# Certificate: Quasiaddition Jordan-identity failure v1

## Statement

The quasiaddition algebra $J^{qa}_\Sigma$ on the Fibonacci model set $\Sigma$ with symmetric window $\Omega=[-\varphi/2,\varphi/2)$ does NOT satisfy the Jordan identity. An explicit minimal counterexample is

$$
x = -2 - 3\varphi \in \Sigma,\quad y = -1 - 2\varphi \in \Sigma,\quad z = \varphi \in \Sigma,
$$
$$
a = e_x + e_y,\quad b = e_z,
$$
$$
a^2 \circ (a \circ b) - a \circ (a^2 \circ b) = e_{-5 - 7\varphi} \ne 0.
$$

This is the smallest counterexample (by lattice coordinate complexity) found by exhaustive search on the symmetric Fibonacci patch with $R=8$.

## Notation

$J^{qa}_\Sigma = \bigoplus_{x\in\Sigma}\mathbb Q\,e_x$ with product
$$
e_x \circ e_y = \begin{cases} e_{x+y} & \text{if } x + y \in \Sigma, \\ 0 & \text{otherwise.}\end{cases}
$$

Extended bilinearly. Lattice notation: $x = (a, b) \leftrightarrow a + b\varphi$; star map: $x^\star = a + b\varphi'$ with $\varphi' = (1-\sqrt5)/2$.

## Dependencies

None classical-theory-level. The certificate is self-contained.

## Proof

The proof is the closed-form calculation in Proposition 6.1 of `Article_001_v1.tex` (`1. Drafts/Article_001/`). Star coordinates of the involved points (rounded to 3 decimals):

| Point | Lattice | Star | In $\Omega = [-0.809, 0.809)$? |
|---|---|---|---|
| $x$ | $(-2,-3)$ | $-0.146$ | yes |
| $y$ | $(-1,-2)$ | $0.236$ | yes |
| $z$ | $(0,1)$ | $-0.618$ | yes |
| $2x$ | $(-4,-6)$ | $-0.292$ | yes |
| $x+y$ | $(-3,-5)$ | $0.090$ | yes |
| $2y$ | $(-2,-4)$ | $0.472$ | yes |
| $2x+z$ | $(-4,-5)$ | $-0.910$ | **no** |
| $x+y+z$ | $(-3,-4)$ | $-0.528$ | yes |
| $2y+z$ | $(-2,-3)=x$ | $-0.146$ | yes |
| $2x+y+z$ | $(-5,-7)$ | $-0.674$ | yes |
| $x+2y+z$ | $(-4,-6)=2x$ | $-0.292$ | yes |
| $3x+z$ | $(-6,-8)$ | $-1.056$ | **no** |
| $3y+z$ | $(-3,-5)=x+y$ | $0.090$ | yes |

Then:

$$
a^2 = e_{2x} + 2 e_{x+y} + e_{2y} = e_{(-4,-6)} + 2 e_{(-3,-5)} + e_{(-2,-4)}.
$$

$$
a \circ b = e_{x+z} + e_{y+z} = e_{(-2,-2)} + e_{(-1,-1)}.
$$

LHS:
$$
a^2 \circ (a \circ b)
= \underbrace{e_{3x+z}}_{=0} + e_{2x+y+z} + 2 e_{2x+y+z} + 2 e_{x+2y+z} + e_{x+2y+z} + e_{3y+z}
$$
$$
= 3 e_{(-5,-7)} + 3 e_{(-4,-6)} + e_{(-3,-5)}.
$$

RHS: 
$$
a^2 \circ b = \underbrace{e_{2x+z}}_{=0} + 2 e_{x+y+z} + e_{2y+z} = 2 e_{(-3,-4)} + e_{(-2,-3)},
$$
$$
a \circ (a^2 \circ b) = 2 e_{2x+y+z} + e_{x+2y+z} + 2 e_{x+2y+z} + e_{3y+z}
= 2 e_{(-5,-7)} + 3 e_{(-4,-6)} + e_{(-3,-5)}.
$$

Residue:
$$
\text{LHS} - \text{RHS} = e_{(-5,-7)} = e_{-5-7\varphi} \ne 0. \quad\square
$$

## Verification of hypotheses

- All star coordinates verified by direct computation $x^\star = a + b\varphi'$.
- Window membership exact: e.g., $\text{star}(2x+z) = -0.910 < -0.809 = -\varphi/2$, so $2x+z \notin \Sigma$.
- No floating-point sensitivity: the discriminations are stable to $> 10^{-3}$, well above double-precision.

## Failure modes / limitations

- The counterexample uses the symmetric window. With the customary half-open window $\Omega = [-1, 1/\varphi)$ the Jordan identity may fail at different specific triples; the algebraic phenomenon is identical because the underlying obstruction is window-induced truncation of monomial paths.
- The "smallest" claim is restricted to lattice-coordinate complexity within the symmetric-window Fibonacci patch at $R=8$. A larger patch with smaller-index counterexamples is not excluded a priori but was not observed.
- The counterexample uses bilinear combinations $a = e_x + e_y$; we have NOT exhibited a single-basis-element failure (and conjecture none exists, because the truncation arguments require at least three independent intermediate sums).
